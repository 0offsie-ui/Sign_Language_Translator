What is Sign Language?

Sign Language is a language that uses visual gestures and signs, as opposed to vocal communication, to convey meaning. It is primarily used by the deaf and hard-of-hearing communities, but is also utilized by individuals with speech or language impairments. Different regions have their own systems.

Why is Sign Language Important?

Sign language is an essential tool for communication for those who cannot hear or speak. It enables expression and participation in society, providing access to education and employment opportunities.

Common Sign Languages

  • American Sign Language (ASL): Used primarily in the United States and Canada.
  • British Sign Language (BSL): Used in the United Kingdom.
  • Filipino Sign Language (FSL): Used in the Philippines.
  • International Sign (IS): Used for international communication.

How Can You Learn Sign Language?

Learning sign language involves practice and immersion. Many schools, online courses, and local communities provide resources, and engaging with sign language users is key.
