What is Sign Language?

Sign Language is a language that uses visual gestures and signs, as opposed to vocal communication, to convey meaning. It is primarily used by the deaf and hard-of-hearing communities, but is also utilized by individuals with speech or language impairments. Sign language is not universal; different regions have their own systems, such as American Sign Language (ASL), British Sign Language (BSL), and Filipino Sign Language (FSL).

Why is Sign Language Important?

Sign language is an essential tool for communication for those who cannot hear or speak. It allows for the expression of thoughts, feelings, and ideas, enabling deaf individuals to engage with others, participate in society, and access education and employment opportunities.

History of Sign Language

The history of sign language dates back to ancient times, with evidence of hand gestures being used in early civilizations. However, modern sign languages began to develop in the 18th and 19th centuries. One of the earliest schools for the deaf, the French National Institute for Deaf-Mutes, was founded by Charles-Michel de l’Épée, who helped establish the foundation for sign language.

Common Sign Languages

  • American Sign Language (ASL): Used primarily in the United States and Canada.
  • British Sign Language (BSL): Used in the United Kingdom.
  • Filipino Sign Language (FSL): Used in the Philippines.
  • International Sign (IS): A type of sign language that is used in international communication between sign language users.

How Can You Learn Sign Language?

Learning sign language involves practice and immersion. Many deaf schools, online courses, and local communities offer resources for learning. Participating in a deaf community and practicing with sign language users is key to becoming fluent.
